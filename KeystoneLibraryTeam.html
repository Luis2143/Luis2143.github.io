Team Roles

Project Lead/Architect

The project lead is someone that has to "see the whole picture", someone who understands how all the puzzle pieces of a team need to fit together to work properly. And in many ways, that also what an architect does, connecting a single building to the community. The role of the project lead is to explore the world of urban design while coordinating the other disciples of the project.

Revit Model Team

Autodesk Revit is a professional 3D modeling software that is used throughout the industry for the past few decades. With recent projects, the Revit team has learned the skills needed to complete this project. And now, this project allows the Revit Model Team to push their mastery to a brand new level.

Street Art/Mural Design Team

Street art is "public-space artwork that's created for consumption outside of the typical art setting." The term "street art" is synonymous with "public art" and can be conveyed through a variety of medians. Including, but not limited to: painting, sculpting, or stained glass.

Website/Infographic Team

A website is an opportunity to formulate what you only have a few minutes to say in an interview/presentation. An infographic The Website team is responsible for composing, and creating a website to fully reflect the design process of the Keystone Library Project

Physical Model Team

A physical model is an excellent way for designers to see three-dimensional representation, and to get a physical feel for how a project will develop. In addition to 3D renderings, building models are another phase of architectural design that can inform how the architect proceeds during the creative/building process.
